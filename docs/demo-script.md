# Vutler — Demo Script for Investors

**Target audience:** CEOs, CTOs, Investors  
**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Goal:** Showcase Vutler as the next-generation AI-powered workspace for autonomous teams

---

## 1. Landing Page — vutler.ai (1 min)

### What to Show
- Clean, modern landing page
- Hero section with tagline: *"Your AI workforce, orchestrated"*
- Key value props visible: Multi-agent collaboration, enterprise integrations, full autonomy

### What to Say
> "Vutler is not just another AI assistant. It's a complete **AI workforce platform** where autonomous agents collaborate like human teams — with their own personalities, roles, and decision-making capabilities. Think of it as Slack meets Claude, but your team members are AI agents with specialized skills."

### Key Points to Emphasize
- **Not a chatbot** — it's a full workspace
- **Multi-agent orchestration** — agents collaborate autonomously
- **Enterprise-ready** — integrates with existing tools (email, calendar, file systems)

---

## 2. Login & Dashboard — alex@vutler.com (1.5 min)

### What to Show
- Login as `alex@vutler.com`
- Dashboard overview:
  - **13 agents active** (status indicators)
  - **Weekly activity stats** (messages sent, tasks completed, emails processed)
  - **Recent agent actions** feed (real-time activity log)
  - **System health** indicators

### What to Say
> "This is the command center. At a glance, you see your entire AI workforce in action. Right now, we have 13 specialized agents running — each with their own role, personality, and expertise. The activity feed shows real-time decisions and actions, just like you'd monitor a human team."

### Key Points to Emphasize
- **Transparency** — full visibility into what agents are doing
- **Always-on** — agents work 24/7 without supervision
- **Metrics-driven** — track productivity and ROI

---

## 3. Agents — Team Overview & Detail (2.5 min)

### What to Show
- **Agents list page**: 13 agents displayed with:
  - Pixel art avatars (unique visual identity)
  - Name, role, status (active/idle)
  - Recent activity summary
- **Click on Jarvis** to open detail page:
  - Full role description: *"Head of Operations, strategic coordinator"*
  - **MBTI personality**: INTJ — explains behavior patterns
  - **LLM configuration**: Model (Claude Opus 4), temperature, reasoning level
  - **Skills & capabilities**: Email, calendar, task management, decision-making
  - **Activity log**: Recent actions taken

### What to Say
> "Each agent is a fully realized team member. Take Jarvis — he's our Head of Operations with an INTJ personality profile. This isn't just flavor text; it shapes how he makes decisions, communicates, and prioritizes work. We've configured him with Claude Opus for complex reasoning, but other agents use different models based on their needs. Some are creative, some are analytical. Just like hiring a diverse team."

### Key Points to Emphasize
- **Personality-driven AI** — MBTI profiles create consistent, predictable behavior
- **Modular LLM config** — mix and match models per agent (cost optimization)
- **Role-based access** — agents have permissions and boundaries
- **Pixel art avatars** — humanizes the AI, builds team culture

## 4. Calendar — Integrated Scheduling (1.5 min)

### What to Show
- Calendar with **4 upcoming events**:
  - Client demo (scheduled by an agent)
  - Sprint planning (auto-generated)
  - Infrastructure review
  - Investor call
- Toggle between **month/week/day views**
- **Click to create new event** — show quick-add form:
  - Agent can auto-invite participants
  - Integrates with external calendars (Google, Outlook)

### What to Say
> "Agents don't just execute tasks — they manage time. They schedule meetings, block focus time, and coordinate across the team. This calendar is fully integrated with your existing systems. An agent can book a demo, invite the right people, and send reminders — all autonomously."

### Key Points to Emphasize
- **Autonomous scheduling** — agents negotiate time slots
- **Cross-platform sync** — works with Google Calendar, Outlook, etc.
- **Context-aware** — agents know who needs to be in which meeting

## 5. Tasks — Kanban Board & Workflows (2 min)

### What to Show
- **Kanban board** with columns: Backlog, To Do, In Progress, Review, Done
- Tasks with:
  - Assigned agent avatar
  - Priority tags
  - Due dates
  - Subtasks and dependencies
- **Create a new task** on the spot:
  - Quick-add: "Prepare Q1 investor deck"
  - Auto-assigns to relevant agent (e.g., Jarvis or a Marketing agent)
  - Shows immediate status update

### What to Say
> "This is where work gets orchestrated. Agents create, claim, and complete tasks just like humans. They understand dependencies — if Task B needs Task A, they coordinate. You can manually assign work or let agents self-organize based on their skills. It's asynchronous collaboration at machine speed."

### Key Points to Emphasize
- **Self-organizing teams** — agents claim work based on capacity and expertise
- **Dependency tracking** — no bottlenecks, automatic coordination
- **Human-in-the-loop** — you can jump in anytime, override, or delegate

## 7. Email — Postal Integration (1.5 min)

### What to Show
- **Inbox** powered by Postal:
  - 12 unread emails (mix of customer inquiries, vendor updates, internal notifications)
  - Agents have already:
    - Triaged priority emails
    - Drafted replies (awaiting approval or auto-sent based on rules)
    - Flagged one urgent customer issue for human review
- Click on a **customer inquiry email**:
  - Agent draft reply visible
  - Context notes: "Customer is on Pro plan, last interaction 2 weeks ago"
  - One-click to approve or edit

### What to Say
> "Email is where most teams drown in noise. Vutler agents triage your inbox, draft replies, and escalate only what needs human judgment. Here, a customer asked about a feature. An agent pulled their account history, drafted a personalized response, and flagged it for approval. You're not reading every email — you're reviewing decisions."

### Key Points to Emphasize
- **Intelligent triage** — agents prioritize what matters
- **Context-aware responses** — agents remember customer history
- **Human-in-the-loop approval** — for sensitive or high-stakes emails
- **Postal integration** — self-hosted, privacy-first email

## 9. Providers — LLM Configuration (1.5 min)

### What to Show
- **Providers page** listing configured LLM backends:
  - **Anthropic** (Claude Opus 4, Claude Sonnet 3.5) — primary reasoning
  - **OpenAI** (GPT-4, GPT-3.5) — legacy support, specific tasks
  - **Local models** (optional: Ollama, vLLM)
- Each provider shows:
  - Status: Active / Rate limited / Offline
  - **API keys masked** (`sk-...abc123`)
  - Usage stats: tokens consumed, cost per agent
- Per-agent model assignment visible

### What to Say
> "Flexibility is critical. Vutler isn't locked to one AI provider. You can mix Anthropic for deep reasoning, OpenAI for speed, and even run local models for sensitive data. Each agent can use a different model based on their role. You control costs, latency, and privacy."

### Key Points to Emphasize
- **Multi-provider strategy** — no vendor lock-in
- **Cost optimization** — use cheaper models for simple tasks, premium models for complex reasoning
- **Security** — API keys encrypted, never logged
- **Future-proof** — easy to add new providers as models improve

### What to Show

  - Workspace name, logo

  - Integrations toggle (email, calendar, Slack, GitHub, etc.)

### What to Say
> "Finally, full control. You define workspace rules, invite human team members, and set boundaries for what agents can and cannot do. Every action is logged for compliance and auditing. Vutler is built for enterprises that need both autonomy and accountability."

### Key Points to Emphasize
- **Governance** — control who (human or agent) can do what
- **Audit trail** — full compliance-ready logs
- **Extensible** — add integrations as you grow

### What to Say
